### NTS

[Theodor Meynert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodor_Meynert), 1833-1892, German-Austrian psychiatrist, neuropathologist, and anatomist, according to the book ["The Role of the Nucleus of the Solitary Tract in Gustatory Processing"](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK1840/), he is the earliest author to name the tract the solitäre Bündel (solitary bundle) in the book "Handbuch der Lehre von den Geweben des Menschen und der Thiere (Handbook of the Doctrine of the Tissues of Humans and Animals, edited by Salomon Stricker)", Chapter 31, Vom Gehirne Aee Säugethiere, The Brains of Mammals, page 789, published in 1872. 

Yet, [Jacob Augustus Lockhart Clarke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_Lockhart_Clarke), [profile](https://makingscience.royalsociety.org/people/na3687/jacob-augustus-lockhart-clarke) from Royal Society, 1817-1880, British physiologist and neurologist who is primarily known for his studies of the brain and the spinal cord, provided the first description of the contribution of the glossopharyngeal nerve to the Solitary tract (The Role of the Nucleus of the Solitary Tract in Gustatory Processing).

[Benedikt Stilling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benedict_Stilling), 1810-1879, a German anatomist, In 1840 he made first mention of vasomotor nerves in a treatise titled Physiologisch-pathologische und medicinisch-praktische Untersuchungen über die Spinal-Irritation. Also, he is remembered for introducing a procedure of serial-section portrayal (thin slicing) of spinal cord specimens for histological study.

[Mihály Lenhossék](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mih%C3%A1ly_Lenhoss%C3%A9k), named often given as Michael von Lenhossék,1863-1937, was a Hungarian anatomist and histologist, he coined the word "Astrocyte" to describe the star-shaped glial cells in the central nervous system, combining the Greek word astron (star) with kytos (hollow receptacle or cell) in 1893.

[Arthur van (or Van) Gehuchten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_van_Gehuchten), 1861-1914, was a Belgian anatomist.
